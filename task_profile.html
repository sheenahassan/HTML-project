STEVE JOBS

About

Steven Paul Jobs (February 24, 1955 – October 5, 2011) was an American businessman, inventor, and entrepreneur best known as the co-founder of Apple Inc. He played a major role in revolutionizing the personal computer, smartphone, and digital music industries. Jobs helped create innovative products such as the Macintosh, iPhone, iPad, and iPod. His vision, creativity, and leadership made Apple one of the most successful technology companies in the world.

Steve Jobs was also the chairman and majority shareholder of Pixar Animation Studios, which produced popular animated films. He is widely regarded as one of the most influential figures in the history of technology and continues to inspire entrepreneurs and innovators around the world.
